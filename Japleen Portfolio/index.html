Dot & Key — Mock Campaign
Skin First. Perfection Never.
A campaign line for a world obsessed with before-and-afters
01 Insight
02 Idea
03 Execution
04 Visuals
Insight
Skincare is sold as transformation. But people don't want to be fixed. They want to be backed.
Big Idea
"Skin First. Perfection Never." Dot & Key as the brand that shows up for your skin as it is, not as an algorithm thinks it should be.
Campaign Tone
Warm but unbothered. Confident without being preachy. Doesn't lecture you about self-love, just lives it.

02
Zomato — Topical Campaign
Teacher's Day. But Make It Food.
Hijacked a classroom concept to say something warm about teachers
01 Insight
02 Idea
03 Execution
04 Visuals
Insight
We all know 'division' from school. But the lunch table division; who gets the last slice, that stayed with us longer.
Big Idea
Use the classroom as a setup. Flip it with food. Distinctly Zomato: witty, warm, never trying too hard.
Campaign Tone
The humour comes first. The sentiment earns its place.
Lead Post — Two Panels
"Math teacher taught us division. 8 ÷ 4 = ... but we still can't divide this fairly."
The equation trails off. The pizza visual completes it. Beat of silence before the punchline — classic two-panel tension.
Social
Caption / Sign-Off
"Happy Teacher's Day to the ones who made every lesson delicious — even the ones that definitely weren't about food."
The "even the ones" clause is the move. It reaches back to the classroom, then lets the warm feeling land.
Social
Alternate Ad Line
"They taught us sharing. We made it optional."
Zomato's brand POV sneaking in — order your own, you've earned it. Teacher's Day as permission for a solo feast.
Social / Print
Push Notification A
"Your math teacher was right about a lot of things. Sharing pizza was not one of them."
Personal, nostalgic, funny. Short enough to read before dismissing. Tap rate bait.
App
Push Notification B
"Teacher's Day. The one day you can eat in class. (Hypothetically.)"
The parenthetical does the work — acknowledges the impossibility while making it feel like a wink.
App
OOH Concept
"A+ in ordering. Average at sharing. Happy Teacher's Day."
Billboard near school areas / office zones. Speaks to the former student in everyone. Hashtag: #NoSharePolicy
OOH
Billboard Concept
"They taught us everything.
We still won't split the bill."

03
Nykaa — Retention Campaign
We're Judging You. Softly.
A win-back campaign that leads with personality, not discount
01 Insight
02 Idea
03 Execution
04 Visuals
Insight
Win-back emails are all the same: "We miss you + 10% off." Nykaa has enough data to actually know what you abandoned. Use it.
Big Idea
"We're Judging You. Softly." A 4-touch sequence that uses playful guilt, personalisation, and Nykaa's natural sass to bring lapsed users back.
Target Window
Users inactive for 20–30 days. Late enough for urgency. Early enough that they haven't actually left.
nykaa@mail.nykaa.com · Day 1 Email
Subject: "Be honest… you forgot us, didn't you?"
Inbox

So… you just stopped showing up?

No warning. No goodbye. Just gone.

Meanwhile, your wishlist is still here. Waiting. Your cart? Emotionally unavailable now. But your last-viewed lip liner? Still waiting to make you look like a baddie.

We're not saying we miss you. (We're absolutely saying we miss you.)

Your glow-up has been on hold since you left. Let's fix that.

Push Notification A/B · Day 3
"Your cart moved on. Should we tell it you're back?"
A/B test against: "You ghosted us. We took it personally." Keep the winner. Kill the other.
App
SMS · Day 5 (still inactive)
"Ghosting us wasn't cute, [Name]. Come back, we'll pretend it never happened."
First-name personalisation + link to last-browsed category. The apology is from them, not you. That's the move.
SMS
In-App Message · Day 7
"Look who decided to show up." → [Wishlist item back in stock / Last category browsed]
The cheeky line only works if personalisation follows immediately.
In-App
Nykaa Privé Variant
"Privé members don't ghost. Yet here we are. Your points expire in 12 days."
Points expiry is a real motivator. The tone stays consistent but the stakes are higher.
Email / Push
Subject Line Options
"This is awkward." / "We need to talk." / "Your cart has trust issues now."
All three tested. Choose by audience segment.
Email
Success Metrics
Open rate · CTR · 14-day reactivation rate · Revenue per reactivated user · Unsubscribe rate (tone check)
If savage tone drives unsubscribes up: soften. If CTR goes up: double down. The data decides the voice.
