Dot & Key — Mock Campaign
Skin First. Perfection Never.
A campaign line for a world obsessed with before-and-afters
01 Insight
02 Idea
03 Execution

Insight
Skincare is sold as transformation. But people don't want to be fixed. They want to be backed.
Big Idea
"Skin First. Perfection Never." Dot & Key as the brand that shows up for your skin as it is, not as an algorithm thinks it should be.
Campaign Tone
Warm but unbothered. Confident without being preachy. Doesn't lecture you about self-love, just lives it.

02
Zomato — Topical Campaign
Teacher's Day. But Make It Food.
Hijacked a classroom concept to say something warm about teachers
01 Insight
02 Idea
03 Execution
04 Visuals
Insight
We all know 'division' from school. But the lunch table division; who gets the last slice, that stayed with us longer.
Big Idea
Use the classroom as a setup. Flip it with food. Distinctly Zomato: witty, warm, never trying too hard.
Campaign Tone
The humour comes first. The sentiment earns its place.
Lead Post — Two Panels
"Math teacher taught us division. 8 ÷ 4 = ... but we still can't divide this fairly."
The equation trails off. The pizza visual completes it. Beat of silence before the punchline — classic two-panel tension.
Social
Caption / Sign-Off
"Happy Teacher's Day to the ones who made every lesson delicious — even the ones that definitely weren't about food."
The "even the ones" clause is the move. It reaches back to the classroom, then lets the warm feeling land.
Social
Alternate Ad Line
"They taught us sharing. We made it optional."
Zomato's brand POV sneaking in — order your own, you've earned it. Teacher's Day as permission for a solo feast.
Social / Print
Push Notification A
"Your math teacher was right about a lot of things. Sharing pizza was not one of them."
Personal, nostalgic, funny. Short enough to read before dismissing. Tap rate bait.
App
Push Notification B
"Teacher's Day. The one day you can eat in class. (Hypothetically.)"
The parenthetical does the work — acknowledges the impossibility while making it feel like a wink.
App
OOH Concept
"A+ in ordering. Average at sharing. Happy Teacher's Day."
Billboard near school areas / office zones. Speaks to the former student in everyone. Hashtag: #NoSharePolicy
OOH
Billboard Concept
"They taught us everything.
We still won't split the bill."

03
Nykaa — Retention Campaign
We're Judging You. Softly.
A win-back campaign that leads with personality, not discount
01 Insight
02 Idea
03 Execution
04 Visuals
Insight
People feel judged for lapsing. Win-backs that lead with discount feel like pity. What if we led with personality instead?
Big Idea
"We're Judging You. Softly." A campaign that calls out the absence with humour and warmth. Not a discount. An invitation back.
Campaign Tone
Gently teasing. Like a friend who notices you've been missing. Funny without being harsh. Inclusive, not condescending.
Email Subject Line
"Where have you BEEN?"
Question, not accusation. Feels like your phone's messaging app, not a brand.
Email
Email Body Opening
"We're not mad. We're just... observant. And we noticed your wishlist is looking a little lonely."
Personality first. Product second. The word "observant" is doing double work — playful but also shows we track their behavior without creeping.
Email
Push Notification
"Your skin care routine called. Said you BOTH abandoned it."
Humour + guilt in one sentence. Blames both customer and product equally. Feels like a friend's joke, not a sales push.
App
Retargeting Ad Copy
"We get it. Life happened. Your skincare didn't though. (Neither did we.)"
Validates their absence. Shows the brand is still there. The parenthetical is the emotional close — we didn't forget you.
Social / Display
SMS Campaign
"Judging less. Offering more. Come back? 👀"
Short enough for SMS. The emoji is key — softens the "judging" joke, makes it a wink instead of a jab.
SMS
Re-engagement Incentive
"Missed you. Not just as a customer. As a person. Here's 15% off everything you were saving."
Humanizes the brand. Discount is earned through warmth, not dangled as bait. References their actual behavior (saved items).
All channels
OOH Concept
"You haven't checked your cart in 47 days.
We're judging you. Softly."
