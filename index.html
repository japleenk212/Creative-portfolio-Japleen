What the work involved
Students ignore posters. They don’t ignore FOMO.
The job wasn’t just posting. It was figuring out what actually gets busy students to show up after classes, societies, assignments, and a million other plans. We learnt quickly that event communication works better when it feels relevant, urgent, and made for students, not like a notice board announcement.
Reels Strategy
I learnt that students don’t stop for effort. They stop for relevance.
The best-performing reels usually had one thing right immediately, a familiar trend, a relatable moment, or a strong hook. Trends worked best when they actually matched the event.
Event Communication
Students don’t stop for information. They stop for something interesting.
Carousels were fun to work with because they let us stretch attention a little longer. Instead of one crowded poster, we could reveal things step by step and make event communication feel less like a notice board.
Platform Thinking / PR & TRUST
Social media quietly became proof that we were organised.
Strong communication helped speakers, guests, and collaborators trust the work before the event even happened. Consistent posting, event coverage, and clear communication made outreach easier because people could actually see the effort behind the society.
